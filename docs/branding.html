The Name

A capn explores, charts, and never sounds the same shoal twice. The pun does real work: the product is literally built on hooks — SessionStart, Stop, post-commit — and the villain wink keeps it from taking itself too seriously.

Vocabulary follows the metaphor and doubles as the API: you chart a route (capn add), you ask the capn (capn ask), stale entries get busted (capn bust), the record is the chart (.capn/entries/). Branding and interface are the same surface — an agent that learns the words has learned the tool.

Marks

1 · The Hook

A fishhook drawn like an anchor's cousin — one stroke, barb in signal red. Reads at 16px favicon size.

2 · The Rose

Compass rose, brass NE quadrant — recall as navigation. More corporate; loses the joke.

3 · The Route

Dotted route ending at X-marks-the-spot: a question finding its file. Best story; weakest as an icon.

Direction: lead with 1 · The Hook; keep The Route as an illustration motif inside docs.

CHART · ASK · BUST
Body — same serif, natural case

Entries whose backing files change are deleted automatically, so when the capn answers, the answer is current.

Interface — monospace, always for commands

capn add "<question>" "<answer>" --files src/api/webhooks.ts

Stack: Iowan Old Style → Palatino → Georgia. Ships-log serif on chart paper, instruments in mono. No webfonts — the brand must survive a README.

Voice

Confident, brief, lightly nautical — one flourish per message, never a costume. The shipped contract is the reference specimen:

Thinking about finding something? Ask the capn first. A hit hands you the answer and the exact files, skipping the whole search. A miss costs seconds; re-exploring costs minutes.
Capn's log before you go: did this session discover any routes worth charting?
